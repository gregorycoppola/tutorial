\section{The Residual Stream vs.\ Propositions: Superposition and the $D_{ff}/D$ Ratio}
\label{sec:superposition}

The formal BP construction uses $D_{\mathrm{model}} = 8$ with one dedicated
dimension per proposition and one OR gate per belief update. A production transformer
has $D_{\mathrm{model}} = 4096$ and $D_{ff} = 14{,}336$: approximately 3.5 OR gates
per residual stream dimension per layer. Why the mismatch?

This section resolves the tension. The formal construction is the minimal case.
Production models generalize it in three ways that each require more hidden units.

\subsection*{Superposition}

Elhage et al.~\cite{elhage2022superposition} show that transformer residual streams
represent far more features than $D_{\mathrm{model}}$ dimensions via superposition.
Features are encoded as nearly-orthogonal directions in a high-dimensional space,
with interference managed by sparsity. A model with $D = 4096$ may be tracking tens
of thousands of distinct concepts simultaneously, each encoded as a direction rather
than a dedicated dimension.

In BP terms, superposition means the residual stream is simultaneously tracking many
factor graph nodes. Each node's belief is encoded as a scalar projection onto a
feature direction, not as a dedicated coordinate. When multiple nodes are active at
once, their representations superpose --- they coexist in the same high-dimensional
vector with manageable interference.

More superposed nodes require more OR gates to update them simultaneously. If the
residual stream tracks $K$ active concepts per token and each needs an independent
belief update per layer, then $D_{ff}$ must be at least $K$. The ratio
$D_{ff} / D \approx 3$--$4$ across Llama, GPT, and other families reflects the
empirical superposition density: each dimension participates in roughly 3--4 active
features at any given time.

\subsection*{$k$-Ary Neighbors}

The formal construction handles pairwise factors: each OR gate takes exactly two
inputs. Real knowledge graph nodes have more than two neighbors. The scaling theorem
of~\cite{coppola2026transformers} handles this: any $k$-ary factor graph binarizes
exactly via a chain of $\lceil \log_2 k \rceil$ pairwise updates. More neighbors
require more hidden units to chain the updates.

\subsection*{Activation Precision}

Sigmoid activation implements the log-odds algebra exactly. ReLU and SwiGLU are
compatible but not exact: they preserve non-negativity but require more hidden units
to achieve the same approximation quality. Production models use SwiGLU
(Llama) or GELU (GPT), which need a larger $D_{ff}$ to match the accuracy of the
exact sigmoid construction.

\subsection*{The Ratio $D_{ff}/D \approx 3$--$4$}

This ratio is an architectural constant, not an accident. It encodes the combined
effect of the three factors above. The minimal formal construction has
$D_{ff}/D = 1$. Production models need $D_{ff}/D \approx 3$--$4$ to handle
superposition, $k$-ary neighbors, and non-sigmoid activations simultaneously.

The ratio is also why the OR evaluations dominate the AND evaluations so completely.
With $D_{ff}/H \approx 416$ for Llama~3 405B, the vast majority of compute is
spent on inference (updating beliefs) rather than routing (finding neighbors). This
is the correct allocation for a system whose primary task is combining evidence from
many sources into coherent conclusions.
